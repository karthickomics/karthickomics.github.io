About

I am a PhD student in Plant Science at KAUST, working at the interface of plant immunity, genomics, and computational biology. I am particularly interested in how we can use large-scale sequencing data, Python-based analysis, and machine learning to understand host–pathogen interactions and improve crop resilience. I am actively exploring postdoctoral and research scientist opportunities in genomics, bioinformatics, and computational biology.

Selected Projects

  • Computational pipelines for plant–pathogen genomics – analysis of large-scale sequencing data to study disease resistance and pathogen diversity.
  • Python- and ML-based approaches for biological data – applying machine learning and statistical models to sequence and expression datasets.
  • Workflow development on HPC systems – designing reproducible analysis pipelines for high-throughput genomics and transcriptomics.
